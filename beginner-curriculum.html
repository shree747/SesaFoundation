What the Master Teaches

Core Skills Taught to Every Beginner

From day one, the master builds confidence, body awareness, and fighting instinct together — not just physical moves.

🧍

Stance & Posture

Standing correctly in Narani Seogi before anything else. Proper posture prevents injury and is the base of every technique.

🤜

Blocks (Makki)

Low, middle, and high blocks — drilled daily to build defence instinct and arm coordination against real attacks.

🦵

Basic Kicks (Chagi)

Front kick, side kick, and roundhouse kick taught step by step: chamber → extend → retract → land.

👊

Punches (Jirugi)

Straight and reverse punches with proper hip rotation and timing — the first real strikes every beginner learns.

📜

Poomsae — Form 1

Taeguk Il Jang: a 17-movement sequence combining stances, blocks, and punches. Trains memory, rhythm, and flow.

🤼

1-Step Sparring

Controlled partner drills — one attack, one defence-and-counter. Builds real-fight instinct in a safe, structured way.

How the Master Teaches

The Master's Teaching Method

"A beginner is a blank canvas. My job is not just to teach kicks — it is to build a body, a mind, and a spirit that can endure anything." — Master, Sesa Foundation Taekwondo

Every technique is first shown at full speed, then broken into slow-motion micro-steps. The master corrects posture, wrist angle, and hip rotation individually — no student is left behind.

Classes use mirror drills for self-correction and count-based repetitions to lock technique into muscle memory. Every session ends with a short reflection circle so students track their own growth.

1

Demonstrate

Full-speed, then slow-motion. Students see the goal first.

2

Break It Down

Split into parts with Korean terminology for each movement.

3

Group Drill

All students drill in lines with count-based repetitions.

4

Personal Correction

Master walks the floor and gives targeted individual feedback.

5

Reflect & Improve

Students state one win and one goal for next session.

Match Preparation

How Beginners Get Ready for Matches

No beginner is rushed into competition. The master follows a clear 4-step path to build confidence safely before any real match.

Step 1 — Weeks 1–3

Taekwondo Sparring Practice

Light controlled sparring inside the taekwondo with full protective gear (helmet, chest guard, gloves, shin pads). Master referees every round personally.

Step 2 — Weeks 4–5

Friendly Matches with Partner Academies

Informal non-graded matches at partner taekwondos. Beginners experience real match atmosphere — a floor, a judge, a clock — for the first time, without pressure.

Step 3 — Week 6–7

Strategy & Video Review

The master reviews each student's sparring on video and gives a personal match plan — scoring zones, preferred combinations, and defensive priorities.

Step 4 — Week 8

Official Beginner Tournament

Students compete in a regional beginner-category tournament. The master coaches cageside. Win or lose, every performance is reviewed and celebrated — then grading for Yellow Belt follows.

Core Values

Respect · Discipline · Focus

These three values are taught alongside every technique — they are what separate a martial artist from someone who just knows how to kick.

🙏

RESPECT

Taught before a single kick. Bowing to the taekwondo, the master, and training partners is a daily practice of honouring effort and humanity.

  • Bow in and out of the taekwondo every session
  • Address the master as Sabeomnim
  • Respect opponents before and after every match
  • Carry respect into daily life outside the taekwondo
⚔️

DISCIPLINE

Built through daily structure — arriving on time, clean uniform, and thousands of deliberate repetitions. Champions are made in discomfort.

  • Never be late to class — no exceptions
  • Dobok (uniform) clean and pressed daily
  • Silence during instruction and drills
  • 30 min home practice every day for grading
🎯

FOCUS

Trained like a muscle. Stillness drills, breathing exercises, and a phone-free taekwondo teach students to direct 100% attention to what matters.

  • Breathing drill (Kibop) opens every session
  • 30-second stillness stand before heavy drills
  • Precision over power — target-point striking
  • Phone-free taekwondo rule — full presence always
